Upgrading to XMB 1.9.8 Engage Upgrading is a one-way process. To backout you will need to restore your files and database from a backup.

Known Issues
These are known issues with the upgrader. However, they will only affect a small percentage of XMB Forum users.
  • Upgrading can be quite resource intensive, especially with large databases.
  • The upgrade script may appear to time out or be inactive. Do NOT stop it or close your browser window.
  • The upgrade script can consume up to two times the amount of disk space you are currently using. Make sure your disk space quota is set accordingly.
  • The upgrade script cannot cope with some indexes. If the upgrader does stop while trying to drop, alter, or add an index, you will need to manually remove all indexes from the corresponding table. Remove as many indexes as possible as the upgrade script will put back in place the one's it needs. After manually dropping all indexes, restart the upgrader.
  • The upgrade script occasionally fails to handle out of order columns, particularly in the settings table. If the upgrader stops while modifying any table, you will need to manually change the table yourself. Once the table is similar to the new table schema, simply re-start the upgrader.
Modifications Notice
This upgrader is designed to work with modifications installed. However, all modification data, templates and code, will be destroyed during the upgrade process. Please proceed with extreme caution if you wish to keep your modification data intact.

Backup Your Forum
Please backup both your forums database and files before continuing. This may seem like common sense but is often forgotten. If the upgrade process fails and you do not have a backup, XMB will be unable to help you. All data could be lost: posts, users, etc. You have been warned!

Upgrading
  1. Disable posting in the settings section of the Administration Panel.
  2. Upload all 1.9.8 files over the top of your existing files.
  3. Delete the /install/ directory and all files in it.
  4. Edit the new config.php file with your details. It may be helpful to reference your old config.php.
  5. Upload the /upgrade/ directory to your forum directory.
  6. Run: http://www.yourdomain.com/path/upgrade/upgrade.php.
  7. Upon completion, verify that the /upgrade/ directory has been removed.
  8. Re-enable posting in the settings section of the Administration Panel.
Enjoy XMB 1.9.8 Engage!
